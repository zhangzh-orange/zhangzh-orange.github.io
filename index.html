Knowledge graph embedding for DTIs prediction

Posted on 2022-09-26

At present, the knowledge graphs used in DTI prediction can be mainly divided into two categories, one is bipartite graph, the other is heterogeneous graph. Bipartite graph only has the relationship information between drug and target; while heterogeneous graph adds more auxiliary information for prediction, but it’s more difficult to find proper methods to embed the heterogeneous graph.

As is shown in the picture on the left, it is difficult for us to know the meaning of a strange word. However, by referring to the picture, we can understand that the word is the name of a football player and has been used to advertise a certain product. “Knowledge” can be formed by making connections between entities like this, and such relationships are the basic form of a knowledge graph.

A true knowledge graph consists of pieces of knowledge, each of which is represented as an SPO triplet.

This is a table of the datasets and relationship types used in this article.

TransE cannot cover a relationship that is not 1-to-1 as it learns only one aspect of similarity. For example, the relationship of locations, Beijing (A) is located in China (B), Shanghai (C) is located in China (B) too, the same relationship connects different entities. TransH give the solution of such problems, as long as finding a hyperplane, which projections of (A, B) and (C, B) on it are the same.

Reference:

​ Mohamed, S.K., Nováček, V. and Nounu, A., 2020. Discovering protein drug targets using knowledge graph embeddings. Bioinformatics, 36(2), pp.603-610.

Chapter 4 Statistical inference

Statistical inference is a method of making decisions about the parameters of a population, based on random sampling.

4.1 Hypothesis testing

4.1.1 Steps of hypothesis testing

4 steps of hypothesis testing:

  1. State the hypotheses (null hypothesis H0 and alternative hypothesis HA).

  2. Deciding significance level and test method (often choose significance levels = 0.01 or 0.05).

  3. Calculating test statistic and P-value.

    The P-value is the probability of observing a sample statistic as extreme as the test statistic, assuming the null hypothesis is true.

  4. Whether to reject the null hypothesis.

4.1.2 Two-Tailed vs. One-Tailed Test

Two-tailed test:

  1. critical area of a distribution is two-sided
  2. whether a sample is greater than or less than a certain range of values

One-tailed test:

  1. critical area of a distribution is one-sided
  2. either greater than or less than a certain value, but not both

According to the testing requirements and professional knowledge, using one-tailed test as far as possible to improve the sensitivity of hypothesis testing.

Type I error is a false positive conclusion；

Type II error is a false negative conclusion.

You decide to get tested for COVID-19 based on mild symptoms. There are two errors that could potentially occur:

4.2 Hypothesis Test for Mean

4.2.1 Hypothesis Test for a Mean

  1. The population variance σ2 is known

When the population variance σ2 is known, no matter whether the sample is large (n>=30) or small (n<30), the distribution of sample mean follows the normal distribution, and after standardization, it follows the standard normal distribution, namely the U distribution. Therefore, the u-test method was used to test the hypothesis.

Example:

The average body length and standard deviation of silver carp larvae raised by conventional methods in a fishery at 1 month old were 7.25cm and 1.58 cm, respectively. In order to improve the quality of larvae, a new method was adopted to raise the larvae. 100 larvae were randomly selected at the age of 1 month, and the average body length was 7.65cm. Is there any significant difference in body length between the new method and the conventional method at 1 month of age?

A z-test is a statistical test to determine whether two population means are different when the variances are known and the sample size is large. A z-test is a hypothesis test in which the z-statistic follows a normal distribution. A z-statistic, or z-score, is a number representing the result from the z-test.

Example:

We know that the average weight of a certain corn is 300g, standard deviation 9.5g. After spraying a plant growth regulator, randomly selected 9 ears of corn, the weights were 308, 305, 311, 298, 315, 300, 321, 294, 320 (g) respectively. Is there any effect of this regulator on ear weight?

  1. The population variance σ2 is unknown

When the population variance σ2 is unknown, as long as the sample size is n >= 30. According to the central limit theorem, the distribution of sample means approximately normal distribution. Therefore, the sample variance s2 can be used to estimate the population variance σ2, and the u test method is still used.

Example:

For the production of certain textiles, the average length of the cotton fibers needs to be more than 30 mm. The average length and standard deviation of a cotton variety with n=400 were 30.2 mm and 2.5 mm, respectively. Is the fiber length of the cotton variety sufficient to produce textile production?
